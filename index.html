Writing your own story

Knowing our collective past can help us change our future. One study found that children who knew more about their family history were more resilient than the kids who did not. Their stories help push us through hard times. Because they did, we know we can too. As members of the church of Jesus Christ we know that this power is a spiritual blessing.

My Grandma Anderson

The greatest lesson I learned from her is Empathy. She was always showing love to those who needed it. Her heart was open to the outcasts and the unloved. She did not deal with bullcrap and really cared about everyone.

John Ellis

John Ellis was one heck of a man. He had a lot of faith and joined the church despite his families hatred toward the church, because of this he was kicked out of his house. This caused him to walk on the frozen Lake Eire to get from his home in Canada to Ohio to join the Saints.
This takes grit. John Ellis was extremely resilient, hardworking and kind. He stayed behind for a time when everyone was leaving for Salt Lake for the sole purpose of helping those Saints that did not have the money to be able to make the journey as well. He has taught me what it means to have grit.

Abigail Evans

From Abigail I have learned courage. Courage is not the lack of fear, but the power to do things despite the fear inside of us. She had many fearful experiences to face. She crossed the plains when she was four years old.
"Her long life was spent in doing good for her fellow men. She had the courage and the endurance of a Spartan. Nothing daunted her."
